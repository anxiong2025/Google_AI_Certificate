01-01 Get the most out of labs

To learn AI, you need to use AI. 

In this certificate, you’ll practice using AI in over 20 hands-on labs designed to mirror real workplace tasks. Each lab includes an expert-led video tutorial and written instructions that you can follow at your own pace. As you progress through the certificate, you’ll build a portfolio of AI projects you can use at work, right away.

How to launch a labThe labs work best when done on a computer. They cannot be completed on the Coursera mobile app. When you reach a lab, click the “Launch lab” button.

The “Launch lab” button will open a new browser tab inviting you to choose how you want to learn:

Select from one of the two options:

  • Seamless login: This option opens a new browser tab pre-loaded with the AI tool so you can practice. Toggle back to the original tab to access the video tutorial and written lab instructions. 

  • Side-by-side learning: This option takes you to a split screen allowing you to practice using AI side-by-side with the video tutorial and written lab instructions below. This is the recommended option. 

    • Which option you choose is up to you, each lab can be completed using either. Just be sure to log into your Google account before you prompt Gemini to save your work.

    How to complete a lab using side-by-side learning

    If you select side-by-side, a split screen window will open: 

  • Left side: A virtual browser with Gemini loaded will open. This is where you will practice using AI. You can save the work you do in the lab by signing into Google. Some labs might require you to log in before you start. If you prefer to use a different AI tool, simply type it into the URL bar. 

  • Right side: You’ll find a video tutorial where your Google AI instructor will take you through each step of the lab. And, below the video tutorial, you’ll find written lab instructions.

    • The written lab instructions include sample prompts, these are highlighted in grey. To use a sample prompt:

  • Copy the prompt: Copy the sample prompt from the lab instructions by selecting the text, right clicking on the selected text, and selecting copy. 

  • Paste the prompt: Paste the sample prompt into Gemini (on the left side of the screen) by right clicking and selecting “Paste”. 

  • Edit the prompt: Edit the text in brackets to personalize your prompt. 

    • Please note, keyboard shortcuts for copy and paste may not work in Coursera labs.

    How to complete a lab using seamless login If you select “seamless login”, two new browser windows will open. One will show the video tutorial and written instructions. The other will load the AI tool for you to practice. Keep both tabs open to follow the written instructions and complete your lab.

    How to use files and documents in your labs 

    Some of the labs will involve uploading files. For example, a report to summarize, or a spreadsheet to analyze. You can use your own files, or use the sample files provided. 

  • To use a sample file: Click the file icon at the top of the lab instructions. Then, click “Use Template” in the top right corner, which will open the file and save it to your Google Drive. You will then be able to use the file via Google drive and/or upload it to the tool you are using.

  • To upload your own file: Click the + symbol in the prompt box, and select “Add from Drive”. 

    • How to finish a lab 

    After you complete every step of the lab, scroll to the end of the written lab instructions and select “Mark as completed.” 

    Once you have completed the lab, close the browser tab to return to the “Launch lab” page. From there, progress to the next item in the course by selecting,  “Go to next item.” 

    Put it to work

    Get the most out of each lab by applying what you learn to your own work.
